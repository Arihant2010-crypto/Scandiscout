Famous Architectural Structures

1. Nyhavn Waterfront Canal 📍

Cultural Significance: 17th-century heritage commercial port district lined with colorful townhouses.

Public Transport Links: Kongens Nytorv Metro Station.

2. Christiansborg Palace 📍

Cultural Significance: Government center housing the Danish Parliament and Supreme Court.

Public Transport Links: Gammel Strand Metro Station.

3. The Round Tower (Rndetårn) 📍

Cultural Significance: 17th-century astronomical observatory featuring a unique equestrian ramp pathway corridor.

Public Transport Links: Nørreport Station.

4. Tivoli Gardens Amusement Park 📍

Cultural Significance: Opened in 1843, one of the world's oldest operating pleasure gardens and theme structures.

Public Transport Links: Copenhagen Central Station (København H).

5. Amalienborg Palace 📍

Cultural Significance: Winter home palace grounds of the Danish royal family consisting of four identical classic palaces.

Public Transport Links: Marmorkirken Metro Station.
